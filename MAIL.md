## The card

- **Moo Luxe Business Card**, 1080gsm, bone `#F4F0E8` front, ink `#141414` back.
- Front: HAYES "H" monogram, debossed if Moo supports it.
- Back: `hayes.press` in Fraunces small caps, nothing else. No name, no phone, no IG.
- Mailer: plain bone envelope, recipient address handwritten in ink pen (not printed). No return address on the outside — bone envelope on a stack of mail reads as invitation, not promo.

The card is *not* a business card. It is the smallest possible printed object that carries the wordmark into a desk or pocket. Treat it that way.

---

## The contact-details question

**Don't put anything on the card beyond `hayes.press`. Not your name, not an email, not initials.**

The reason:

- A card that says `— PJ` is a **calling card**. It announces: "a person made this, the person is named PJ." Every recipient then asks "who is PJ?" — and the answer is you, a Stripe-adjacent SV person, which collapses the brand posture ("a house that publishes") into "a dude's side project." The whole editorial voice depends on the first impression being institutional, not personal.
- A card that says only `hayes.press` is a **found object**. It works identically in three very different contexts: (a) a personal envelope from you to Kyle Chayka, (b) a stack of 30 dropped by Nick in a NYC cafe, (c) handed out by Cowan at a Maak Haus party. In all three, the URL is the whole ask. The object doesn't have to work harder than that.
- If someone loves it, they already have the mechanism to tie it back: they go to `hayes.press`, they find the Inquire form, they write. Anyone who'd actually engage on this level will do this in ~30 seconds. The card doesn't need to do the work for them.
- The personal tie happens in the **handwritten note**, not on the card. The note is where "— PJ" lives, and the note only goes in mailed/personal envelopes — never with ambient drops.

**Rule**: the card is anonymous. The note is personal. Ambient drops get no note. Mailed envelopes get a note. That's the whole logic.

## What not to do

- ❌ Don't enclose a letter. The envelope is the letter.
- ❌ Don't include a QR code. The URL is the QR code.
- ❌ Don't mention Volume 01 / the release date / the price. They will find it.
- ❌ Don't follow up on the card. If they ask, you respond. Otherwise silent.
- ❌ Don't sign the note with anything but "— PJ". No title, no handle, no company.
- ❌ Don't send the card to anyone whose address you had to guess. If you're not sure, skip them. A wrong address returns the card to sender and breaks the spell.
