# HAYES — Volume 01 Launch Plan

> Companion to `DISTRO.md`. This is the execution doc — drafts, contacts, cadence, todos.

---

## 0. The premise (what we're actually doing)

HAYES is a parody fashion brand dressed as a real one. The site is the product. There is no inventory and no warm network. The job of the launch is **placement, not reach**: get the right 50 people to know it exists.

Success at 6 weeks looks like:
- 20–30 inbound inquiries (tier doesn't matter — signal is the ask)
- 3–5 earned editorial mentions (tweet, Substack aside, newsletter blurb)
- 1 canonical node (Spyplane / Derek Guy / Chris Black / Nasso) that becomes the reference
- Are.na channel with real connection activity
- Japanese visitors finding the `/ja` mirror organically

**Success is not:** follower count, likes, press impressions, "going viral." If a tweet goes viral, we ride it; we do not chase it.

## 1. How long do we do this for?

**Active launch: 6 weeks (Apr 22 – Jun 1, 2026).**

| Phase | Window | Posture |
|---|---|---|
| Week 0 — Tier 0 hand-off | Wed Apr 22 | Nasso. Everything else silent. |
| Week 1 — Tier 1 send | Apr 23–26 | Six cold emails, one IG post/day, card drops begin. |
| Week 2 — Monitor + widen | Apr 27 – May 3 | Tier 2 warm seeds. IG continues. Watch for replies. |
| Week 3 — JA pass | May 4–10 | Popeye / Houyhnhnm / Kamoshita circle. |
| Week 4 — Tier 3 broad | May 11–17 | Newsletter-style broad send. |
| Week 5 — Read the room | May 18–24 | Measure. Decide if Vol 01 caught or is slow-burn. |
| Week 6 — Wrap + cooldown | May 25 – Jun 1 | Stop active outreach. Let it sit. |

**After 6 weeks: stop pushing.** Editorial brands are built over years, not weeks. Pestering past week 6 makes HAYES look desperate — which is the one tone the voice cannot survive. Shift to **monthly cadence**: one considered IG post per week, one journal entry on hayes.press per month, hand-to-hand cards at dinners/events as life allows.

**Volume 02 (Autumn MMXXVI) is the next real moment.** Target: early Sep 2026. Between Jun and Sep, HAYES is a slow-burn. The gap between volumes is part of the conceit — a house that publishes twice a year.

**Exit conditions that override the 6-week plan:**
- **If a Tier 1 lands hard in week 1:** pause Tier 2, ride the moment. Reply, thank, don't re-pitch.
- **If nothing lands by week 4:** don't panic. The half-life of a Spyplane mention or a Derek Guy QT is months, not days. Keep the cadence; extend Tier 2 trickle to week 6.
- **If the parody is mistaken for sincerity at scale:** write a short note on the site (a "letter from the editors") that leans deeper into the conceit rather than breaking it. Never explain the joke.

## 10. Groundswell mechanics (why this can work)

Three layers to the distribution:

- **Layer 1 (the sharers):** Tier 1 + Nasso. ~10 people total. These are the tastemakers whose single mention moves the rest of the graph. **They must know it's a parody** — the whole point is that they find it funny and place it correctly. The homepage + Antecedents read as parody to anyone paying close attention; the voice gives them cover to quote it.
- **Layer 2 (their audience):** Spyplane's 40k readers, Derek Guy's 1M followers, HLG's listeners. They encounter HAYES through the tastemaker's framing. **They do not need to know it's a parody.** Many will take it at face value — which is fine. The editorial voice survives either reading.
- **Layer 3 (the long tail):** Japan, editorial-adjacent readers, future Vol 02 subscribers. Found through Are.na, Google, hreflang routing, and organic. **Neither parody nor sincerity matters here** — they're here for the atmosphere.

Does Nasso need to know it's a parody? **Yes.** Telling him explicitly ("It's a parody. Tell me if it's funny.") gives him the mental permission to share it without worrying he's been duped. If he mistakes it for a sincere brand he won't post about it — sincere brands are off-topic for his bit.

Does Derek Guy need to know? **Probably yes, but Antecedents will telegraph it.** The copy makes the parody register legible to anyone with taste. Worst case: he takes it at face value and the menswear graph still lands.
