# HAYES — Distribution Strategy

> Volume 01, Summer MMXXVI. Drafted 2026-04-17.

## The premise

HAYES is not DTC. The goal of distribution is **not reach — it is placement**. Fifty of the right people knowing HAYES exists is worth more than fifty thousand of the wrong ones. The ask is never "buy." It is "here."

The product is a conceit as much as a garment: a house that publishes, an atelier that corresponds, a periodical that ships twice a year. This works on editorial channels. It fails on broadcast channels.

**Success metric for Volume 01 distro:**
- 20–30 inquiries from people with taste (not volume)
- 3–5 earned editorial placements
- 1 "canonical" mention (Spyplane / Derek Guy / Popeye) that becomes the reference
- A JA-reading audience that discovers the mirror site

Not: follower count, tweet likes, press impressions.

## Sequencing

### Week 1 — Apr 20–26 (finalize, don't send)
- **Mon Apr 20:** Close the Tier 1 list at 6. Pull email addresses, verify current affiliations, check for any recent moves (people change mastheads).
- **Tue–Wed:** Draft 6 individual emails. Each one personalized in a real way — reference something they wrote in the last 90 days. This is the work.
- **Thu Apr 23:** Send Tier 1 emails between 9–11am ET, staggered. Tuesday/Wednesday mornings are peak open rates but Thursday AM is less crowded.
- **Fri:** Silence. Do not follow up.

### Week 2 — Apr 27–May 3 (monitor + widen)
- **Mon:** Review Tier 1 replies. Reply same day if you get them.
- **Tue–Fri:** Start Tier 2 rollout — 5 emails/day, in batches, personalized.
- If any Tier 1 publishes/posts: leverage in Tier 2 sends ("you may have seen Spyplane's note — here it is first-hand").

### Week 3 — May 4–10 (JA pass)
- Send JA-specific pitches to Popeye / Houyhnhnm / Kamoshita circle. Lead with the translation as the story (it *is* a story — most EN brands don't bother with a proper mirror).
- Japan coverage lags 2–4 weeks but is durable; it's the long payoff.

### Week 4 — May 11–17 (Tier 3 + measurement)
- Send Tier 3 broad note.
- Measure: inquiries, DMs, press hits, site analytics.
- Decide: is Volume 01 a slow-burn or did it catch? Adjust Volume 02 launch accordingly.

### Month 2 onward — maintenance cadence
- One meaningful update per month. Not more. The editorial voice demands rarity.
- Physical cards carried and given hand-to-hand at Pitti, fashion week, dinners. Ambient distribution.

## Channels matrix

| Channel | Use | Don't use |
|---|---|---|
| **Personal email** | Primary. Every Tier 1/2 send. | — |
| **Twitter/X** | Reply, don't broadcast. Engage with Tier 1 posts thoughtfully, in-character. | Don't tweet cold into a zero-follower account. |

| **Physical cards** | Hand-to-hand. Never in bulk. | Mass mail. |
| **Print** | Long-term: a physical zine of Volume 01 mailed to Tier 1. Expensive but on-brand. | — |

## What NOT to do

- ❌ Tweet today, Friday afternoon, with zero followers
- ❌ Buy a newsletter feature / sponsored post — detectable, off-voice
- ❌ DM Tier 1 on Twitter (goes to message-request hell)
- ❌ Send press releases. HAYES doesn't have a PR department. Personal email or nothing.
- ❌ "Launch week" energy. The brand is deliberately quiet. Match the channel to the voice.
- ❌ Generate more card mockups before real ones arrive
- ❌ Follow up more than once on Tier 1 emails (dignified silence > pestering)
